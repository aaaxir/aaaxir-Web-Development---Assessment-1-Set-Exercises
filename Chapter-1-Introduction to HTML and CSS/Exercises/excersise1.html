Thalassery Biryani

A fragrant and flavorful South Indian biryani originating from the city of Thalassery in Kerala. This biryani is known for its unique blend of spices and the use of small-grain, fragrant rice.

Ingredients

  • 500g basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup ghee or clarified butter
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional garnish)
  • Fried onions (optional garnish)

Method

  1. Heat ghee and oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the whole spices (fennel seeds, cumin seeds, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon) and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  5. Now add the chicken pieces and cook until they are browned.
  6. Whisk the yogurt with turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Add this mixture to the pot and mix well.
  7. Cover the pot and let the chicken cook until tender, stirring occasionally.
  8. Meanwhile, in a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt and soaked rice. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% done. Drain and set aside.
  9. Once the chicken is cooked and the gravy is thick, layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken gravy.
  10. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
  11. Once done, gently mix the rice and chicken together.
  12. Serve hot, garnished with fresh coriander leaves and fried onions if desired.
